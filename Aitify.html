An intuitive, fast-to-master website for generative AI image creation tools. It enables users to replace command-line inputs with a graphical interface, thereby harnessing generative language models to fulfill using requirements.

Background

After ChatGPT came out in 2022, many AI tools for generating content appeared quickly. But their interfaces are very different from each other, and they can be hard to use. There hasn’t been enough research on how to design these AI interfaces, so it’s difficult to make all their complex features easy and clear for users.

Process

Following the non-linear process of design thinking, this outline describes the workflow of my research project. During the ideation, testing, and validation phases, the project underwent two complete iterations and innumerable rapid, detailed refinements.

In total, the study comprised hands-on sample-operation experiments involving 90 participants.

Research

Overview of the Applications Dominating the Market

At the beginning of 2024, I conducted a written study of the applications market and concluded that the main image-generative application tools available at that time could be categorized into the following three types based on the services they offered:

Only offers simple, intuitive generation features. It does not include any further model adjustments or detailed image retouching.

Provides detailed controls for adjusting the model. You can fine-tune image-generation settings and numbers with a high level of flexibility, all through an easy-to-use interface.

Offers various design tools for fine-tuning images. The layout and workflows are very similar to Figma.

Motivations and Selection Factors for Using AI Image Generators

I also invited 30 experienced users, selected by convenience sampling, to complete a survey on AI Image Generator usage.The results indicate that, compared to directly using AI-generated images, using them as creative inspiration is a more common motivation.

When choosing a specific AI image generator tool, aside from brand recognition and free access—two major commercial and market considerations— How user-friendly the interface is became the primary deciding factor.

Key impressions from the interviews

After picking three representative market samples of AI image generator, I designed tasks, had thirty participants perform them, and used semi-structured interviews to understand their actual impressions.

“There are many features I don’t understand. If there were numerical indicators, I might be able to get a rough idea, but sinceI can’t decipher them, I can only click around randomly.”

“There are too many elements on the screen to manipulate, and nothing stands out. Even though I read the text prompts, my attention keeps getting pulled away by everything else.”

Key Findings

Defined

Through user research and semi-structured interviews, three distinct personas were identified, categorized by their level of experience with generative AI drawing platforms: Lin Ming-Xuan, a beginner; Huang Hui-yu, a detail-oriented intermediate user; and Chen Zhi-hao, an advanced user with frequent usage habits.

User Journey map

By synthesizing these personas and referencing insights from the research process, a realistic user journey map was developed. This map includes visualized emotional curves and highlights opportunity points at each stage of the journey.

Identified Pain Points & Goals

How might we help users quickly fulfill their image generation goals using generative AI platforms?

How might we reduce the learning cost of using the platform's features?

How might we further enhance users’ confidence and satisfaction when operating the system?

Let everyone easily learn and flexibly use AI image generators.

Develop

Design strategies & Prototypes verification and iteration

Design strategies were developed based on the pain points and goals identified during the research phase, beginning with a clear definition of design variables and supported by design theory (based on F-pattern layout and color psychology).

User Experience Test - Usability Testing

The user experience evaluation at this stage aims to assess the effectiveness and satisfaction of the prototypes through a combination of usability testing, the System Usability Scale (SUS), and a user interaction satisfaction questionnaire.

Between-Group Experiment and SUS Analysis

A set of primary user tasks was designed based on core operations of AI image generator websites. Using convenience sampling, a total of 60 participants were recruited to conduct a between-group experimental design, with each prototype tested by a unique group of 10 participants.

The experimental results were quantified and analyzed. Differences in user satisfaction across different prototype versions were identified using the SUS score.

Interview keywords summary

Based on the usability testing of six sample tools and semi-structured interviews, key insights were extracted using an affinity diagram approach to guide further iterations.

Results and Iterations

The next stage of iteration will focus on combining the strengths of these two samples. Detailed adjustments will be made based on the emotional responses, effective features, and operation processes identified through interview keywords.

Top-Performing Samples

In the usability test, C’s homepage and Sample F’s fine-tuning feature page received the highest satisfaction ratings. They also outperformed the other samples in task performance.

Overall, the design of these two samples on their respective feature pages was found to be more intuitive and easier to understand for users. This helped users feel more confident when using the site and also improved their learning speed and comprehension.

Summary & Reflection

What I learned from this?

Throughout this research, I conducted 30+60 in-person usability tests with different samples. These physical sessions revealed that beyond the speed of website operations and on-screen behaviors, face-to-face testing allows for observing more subtle and meaningful user reactions beyond just mouse movements.

Users' facial expressions and body language provided rich information—frowning, hesitation, uncertainty, and confusion were clearly visible.

During follow-up interviews, when I asked about these emotional moments, users were eager to share their experiences and thoughts. These emotional insights became one of the most valuable outcomes of the experiment.

Additionally, this process reaffirmed my belief that interface design plays a crucial role in making technology accessible. As technology grows more complex, intuitive interfaces are essential for allowing everyone to benefit from new advancements.
